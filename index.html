The Works of Tarkovsky

These are some of my favorite films by my favorite director, Andrei Tarkovsky

The Mirror (1975)

A dreamlike reflection into memories

In my opinion, The Mirror is Tarkovsky's magnum opus. A film that transcends story telling, it's a semi-autobiographical film that deals with memories. The film uses non-linear story telling in a way that perfectly grabs the essence of remembering your past. Combined with Tarkovsky's signature distinct visuals, the film is an experience that needs to be rewatched multiple times to grasp.

Andrei Rublev (1996)

A biography on Russia's iconic painter

An absolutely gorgeous look into the life of Andrei Rublev, one of Russia's most revered painters. At a 3 hour run time, the film interweaves religion and spirituality with art. The film is beautiful, yet brutal. Epic in scale, if it wasn't for Tarkovsky's masterful directing, it would be hard to get through, but there are not many directors that can pull off a film like this.

Stalker (1979)

A Sci-Fi Masterpiece

Tarkovsky only directed 7 films before his death, and this is the film that contributed to his death. The science fiction film deals with a team of explorers that venture into a Zone no one is allowed in with the hopes of their deepest desires will be granted inside the mysterious Zone. The film was filmed was shot in a location near a chemical plant which contaminated a lot of the area and poisoned the crew. Tarkovsky, his wife, and lead actor Tolya Solonitsyn all died of cancer years later.
